April 29th, 2023
I've been a huge proponent in developing interconnecting systems that can all talk to each other in ways that allow for a greater degree of choice by the end user, and something that seemingly checks all of those boxes is... Crypto. Now, before you tell me all of the negatives of crypto, let me tell you all of the positives, because I want to make you forget the negatives so I can tell you them again after the positives. First of all, everything is public, meaning anything shady has publicly viewable log of the transactions, and the amounts being traded. Another positive is the permanence of stuff like the blockchain, and the fact that crypto is essentially undeletable, meaning that no matter how low the market crashes, it can never truly die. NOW ON TO THE NEGATIVES: Everything is public, meaning anything that you wouldn't want to be public can be sniffed out and tracked by anyone who knows your wallet ID. The blockchain being permanent, while allowing for all sorts of good in terms of permanence, creates issues when any kind of bug comes up and the things you've minted become completely unusable for their intended purpose. Along with that, making everything have resale value is a really bad idea, because in general, it attracts almost exclusively people who want to make a profit off of something that should also serve either a functional purpose, or a ludic purpose.

Yume Nikki is an atmospheric horror game which was first released to the public (This might be the date of the full finished release, I'm not sure) in 2018, produced by Kikiyama (stylized as KIKIYAMA. This game stars Madotsuki as the protagonist, who is a shut-in (there's a Japanese term that describes it better than that, but since I'm offline, I can't search for the *actual* name), and goes into a world of her dreams, where your main goal (as described by Kikiyama) is to find all of the effects, which as the name implies, affect Madotsuki in some way, or give her an item such as a kitchen knife, or a bicycle. She goes to different worlds through a place called the Nexus, in which there are several doors that she can go through, and within those doors, there are worlds that can lead to other worlds, and so-on and so-forth (This worlds-within-worlds concept is the foundation for the Yume 2kki fangame). I like the visual style of the game, which that there is none (In all seriousness, the art style is over the place, but its quite surreal in a lot of parts).
